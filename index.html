A recent entrepreneurial endeavor.

This past year on student government, I championed solar panels in school parking lots, speaking to city representatives and the school council about the boons of energy efficiency. As the student representative to school committee meetings, I engaged in a Newton South initiative to erect solar panels in school parking lots. The build period occurred during the school year, and I was tasked with coordinating the relocation of parking- choosing the parking spots that maintained safety during busy entrance and exit times. We ended up relocating some parking spots onto the grass and paving parts of the curb, and we also engineered novel parking spots after measuring specific road widths and assessing their safety. Today, the solar panels stand tall above school parking lots, reducing the school’s energy footprint by over 30%. The parking report generated is below.

A student government initiative.

As a rising sophomore, I worked at Newton City Hall under the Economic Development Director Nancy Hyde. Specifically, I was tasked with redesigning city parking requirements for local restaurants, and I wrote multiple reports and conducted a study that culminated in a final ordinance amendment. Throughout the process, I employed mathematics to model the population density of Newton Center as a function of time and myriad other variables, and then engineered parking requirements that minimized the cost to restaurants while concurrently ensuring citizen safety. What amazed me most about this experience was the capacity for an individual person- me, that is- to produce change within our city. It truly convinced me that the government is for the people, by the people, and that with science, technology, and determination, policy can change.


Effecting Local Policy Change
